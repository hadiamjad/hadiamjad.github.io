Abdul Haddi Amjad


 

Amjad is a Ph.D. student at Virginia Tech, advised by Dr. Muhammad Ali Gulzar from Virginia Tech and Dr. Zubair Shafiq from UC Davis. His research focuses on using software engineering techniques to tackle internet security and privacy challenges. Despite the widespread use of privacy-enhancing technologies like adblockers, their effectiveness often relies on human processes that are error-prone and not scalable across millions of websites and apps over time. Amjad's research aims to address these limitations by identifying the pain-points of privacy-enhancing technologies and building automated frameworks using software engineering techniques, such as automated-fault localization. He specifically addresses the challenge of blocking mixed web resources that perform both tracking and functional activity on websites without disrupting their intended functionality. Through his work, Amjad aspires to make the web a more private and secure place.
